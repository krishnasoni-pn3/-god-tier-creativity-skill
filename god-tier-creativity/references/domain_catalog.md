# Cross-Pollination Domain Catalog

> Use this catalog during **Step 3: Cross-Pollination** of the Cognitive Scaffolding. Pick a domain that is maximally unrelated to your prompt. Extract its principles. Force a collision.

---

## 1. Natural Sciences

| Domain | Key Principles | Cross-Pollination Spark |
|---|---|---|
| **Mycology** | Mycelium networks share resources without central control; fungi decompose the old to feed the new; spores travel vast distances to colonize new territory | Design a social network where content spreads like spores — no algorithm, just environmental conditions |
| **Marine Biology** | Bioluminescence as communication in darkness; pressure adaptation at extreme depths; coral builds structure from accumulated tiny organisms | Design a productivity tool that works better under pressure, like deep-sea organisms |
| **Volcanology** | Explosive release after long pressure buildup; lava creates new fertile land; magma chambers are hidden reservoirs of energy | Design a brainstorming tool based on magma chambers — ideas build pressure underground before erupting |
| **Crystallography** | Order emerges from chaos through slow cooling; seed crystals template entire structures; impurities create the most beautiful gems | Design an education platform where "impurities" (mistakes) create the most valuable learning gems |
| **Entomology** | Swarm intelligence without central leadership; metamorphosis as radical self-reinvention; pheromone trails as invisible communication | Design a project management tool where tasks self-organize like ant colonies |
| **Ornithology** | Murmuration — thousands moving as one without a leader; nest-building as architectural art from found materials; dawn chorus as coordinated performance | Design a collaborative music platform where individual inputs create murmurations of sound |
| **Botany** | Phototropism — growing toward light; roots communicate through chemical signals; deciduous trees strategically shed to survive winter | Design a career platform that helps people grow toward their "light" — shedding what doesn't serve them |
| **Glaciology** | Glaciers carve landscapes over millennia; ice cores store ancient atmosphere; calving — dramatic break-off events after slow accumulation | Design a personal finance app where savings "carve" your financial landscape like glaciers |
| **Astrobiology** | Life finds a way in extreme environments; panspermia — life traveling between worlds; habitable zones as "Goldilocks" conditions | Design a dating app based on habitable zones — matching people in their Goldilocks overlap |
| **Seismology** | Tectonic plates build pressure at fault lines; P-waves arrive before the main shock (early warning); liquefaction turns solid ground into fluid under stress | Design a team management tool that detects "P-waves" — early warning signs of burnout |

## 2. Physics & Mathematics

| Domain | Key Principles | Cross-Pollination Spark |
|---|---|---|
| **Fluid Dynamics** | Laminar vs turbulent flow; vortices concentrate energy; fluids follow the path of least resistance | Design a city navigation app where pedestrian flow is optimized like laminar fluid |
| **Topology** | A donut and a coffee cup are the same shape; surfaces can be twisted without tearing; Möbius strips have only one side | Design a website where navigation is topological — every page connects to every other without hierarchy |
| **Quantum Mechanics** | Superposition — existing in multiple states until observed; entanglement — instant correlation across distance; tunneling through barriers | Design a decision-making tool where options exist in superposition until you "observe" (commit) |
| **Chaos Theory** | Butterfly effect — tiny changes cascade; strange attractors create order within chaos; fractal boundaries between states | Design a creative writing tool where small word changes cascade into entirely different story outcomes |
| **Acoustics** | Resonance amplifies specific frequencies; standing waves create stable patterns; echoes carry information about space shape | Design a feedback system where resonance amplifies the right behaviors naturally |
| **Optics** | Lenses focus by bending light; prisms decompose white light into spectra; total internal reflection traps light inside fiber | Design a learning platform that acts as a prism — decomposing complex skills into their spectral components |
| **Thermodynamics** | Entropy always increases; heat flows from hot to cold; phase transitions happen at critical thresholds | Design an organization tool where entropy is the enemy — actively fighting the second law of disorder |
| **Fractal Geometry** | Self-similarity at every scale; infinite complexity from simple rules; coastlines get longer the more closely you measure them | Design a task manager where each task reveals fractal sub-tasks the closer you look |

## 3. Human Body & Medicine

| Domain | Key Principles | Cross-Pollination Spark |
|---|---|---|
| **Neurology** | Neuroplasticity — the brain rewires itself; synaptic pruning removes unused connections; mirror neurons fire when observing others | Design a habit tracker that mimics synaptic pruning — unused habits are actively removed |
| **Immunology** | Antibodies are custom-built for each threat; immune memory prevents repeat infections; autoimmune disorders attack the self | Design a cybersecurity tool that builds custom "antibodies" for each type of threat it encounters |
| **Forensic Pathology** | Every contact leaves a trace (Locard's principle); decomposition reveals timeline; cause vs manner of death distinction | Design a code review tool based on Locard's principle — every developer leaves a forensic trace |
| **Embryology** | Cells differentiate from identical to specialized; morphogen gradients guide development; folding creates 3D structure from flat sheets | Design an onboarding system where new hires differentiate from generalists to specialists via gradient signals |

## How to Use This Catalog

1. **Random Selection**: Close your eyes and point. The less related the domain feels, the better.
2. **Principle Extraction**: Don't use the domain as a metaphor. Extract the *mechanism* and apply it structurally.
3. **Force the Collision**: The discomfort you feel when combining unrelated domains is the signal that originality is near.
