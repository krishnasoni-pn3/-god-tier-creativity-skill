# Naming Frameworks & Patterns

> Every concept deserves a name that IS creative work. Generic labels ("Option 1", "Concept A") are FORBIDDEN. Use these frameworks to generate names that tell stories.

---

## 1. Naming Frameworks

### The Metaphor Transfer
**How it works:** Borrow a name from a completely unrelated domain. The metaphorical distance creates intrigue and layers of meaning.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Amazon** (river → marketplace) | Vast, flowing, everything-carrying, unstoppable |
| **Apple** (fruit → technology) | Simple, natural, approachable — the opposite of "tech" |
| **Shell** (seashell → energy) | Something precious found in nature, containing hidden power |
| **Patagonia** (region → clothing) | Wild, remote, untamed — the brand IS the destination |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Glacier** — a long-term savings platform (slow, massive, unstoppable accumulation)
- **Anvil** — a code review tool (where raw code gets hammered into shape)
- **Lighthouse** — a mentorship platform (guiding through darkness, visible from afar)
- **Undertow** — a deep work app (the invisible current that pulls you below the surface)

---

### The Sensory Name
**How it works:** Choose a word that triggers a physical sensation. The name should be felt, not just read.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Snap** (Snapchat) | You hear the click. You feel the speed. |
| **Crush** (soda brand) | Physical pressure, sweetness, intensity |
| **Slack** (workplace tool) | Physical relaxation, looseness, relief from tension |
| **Crisp** (various brands) | Tactile sharpness, freshness, clarity |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Murmur** — a quiet social network (the sound of intimate conversation)
- **Ember** — a journaling app (warm, glowing, about to catch fire)
- **Silt** — a data analytics tool (fine particles that settle and reveal the landscape beneath)
- **Tremor** — a notification system (you feel it before you hear it)

---

### The Compound Myth
**How it works:** Collide two unexpected words to create a compound that feels mythological — bigger than its parts.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Thunderbird** (thunder + bird) | Mythic power, natural force, ancient and airborne |
| **Firefox** (fire + fox) | Elemental danger and animal cunning combined |
| **Starbucks** (star + Starbuck) | Celestial and literary — the first mate from Moby Dick |
| **Snapdragon** (snap + dragon) | Delicate flower hiding a dragon's jaw |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Ironbloom** — a manufacturing startup (industrial strength producing beauty)
- **Nightglass** — a meditation app (looking through darkness, finding clarity)
- **Stonewind** — an architecture firm (permanent material shaped by invisible force)
- **Ashveil** — a privacy tool (what remains after the fire, hidden behind a veil)

---

### The Scientific Poetry
**How it works:** Take a scientific or technical term and use it as a name. The term should sound beautiful while carrying a precise meaning that maps to the product.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Nebula** (cloud of gas in space) | Vast, luminous, birthplace of stars |
| **Helix** (spiral structure) | DNA, spiraling upward, interconnected |
| **Quartz** (mineral / crystal) | Precision, clarity, natural geometry |
| **Flux** (continuous change) | Movement, adaptability, flow |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Parallax** — a perspective-shifting design tool (things shift depending on your viewpoint)
- **Mycelium** — a networking platform (underground connections that feed the whole forest)
- **Vestige** — a digital archaeology tool (traces of what existed before)
- **Perihelion** — a goal-tracking app (the closest point to the sun — your peak moment)

---

### The Action Verb
**How it works:** Name the product after what the user DOES with it. The name is an imperative — a command to act.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Zoom** (move fast) | Speed, directness, closing distance instantly |
| **Sprint** (short burst of maximum effort) | Intensity, speed, defined duration |
| **Notion** (a thought, an idea) | The spark before action. The seed of something. |
| **Figma** (from "figment") | Imagination made tangible |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Forge** — a project building tool (heat + hammer = creation)
- **Carve** — a writing tool (removing material to reveal the sculpture inside)
- **Anchor** — a habit tracking app (hold your position, resist the current)
- **Ignite** — a startup accelerator (the moment of combustion)

---

### The Negative Space
**How it works:** Name something by what's absent, empty, or void. Absence creates intrigue and invites the user to fill the gap.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Void** (various brands) | Emptiness that contains infinite possibility |
| **Zero** (various brands) | The starting point. Nothing, from which everything comes. |
| **Null** (programming concept) | Intentional absence. Powerful in its emptiness. |
| **Blank** (various brands) | A canvas waiting to be filled. You complete it. |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Hollow** — a meditation app (empty yourself, find what remains)
- **Absence** — a digital detox tool (the product is about NOT using it)
- **Lacuna** — a gap-analysis tool (Latin for "gap" — finding what's missing)
- **Stillpoint** — a focus app (the moment between breaths where everything stops)

---

### The Cultural Artifact
**How it works:** Name something after a figure, place, or object from mythology, history, or literature. The name carries centuries of accumulated meaning.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Nike** (Greek goddess of victory) | Winning is built into the name |
| **Atlas** (Titan who held up the sky) | Strength, endurance, holding everything together |
| **Oracle** (prophetic figure) | Seeing the future, providing answers |
| **Pandora** (Greek myth) | Opening something that can't be closed — curiosity, consequence |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Cassandra** — a predictive analytics tool (she saw the future; nobody believed her)
- **Babel** — a translation service (the tower where all languages diverged)
- **Prometheus** — an open-source framework (stole fire from the gods and gave it to humanity)
- **Labyrinth** — a complex project management tool (navigating complexity, finding the center)

---

### The Sound-First Name
**How it works:** Choose a name primarily for how it SOUNDS when spoken. Meaning is secondary to phonetic pleasure. The mouth-feel matters.

| Real-World Examples | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Kodak** (invented word) | Hard K sounds feel bold, decisive, mechanical |
| **Häagen-Dazs** (completely made up) | Sounds European, luxurious, exotic — means nothing |
| **Xerox** (from "xerography") | Sharp, unusual consonant cluster — instantly memorable |
| **Rolex** (invented) | Rolling R, luxurious L, precise X — sounds expensive |

**Generated Examples:**
- **Kvell** — a celebration/recognition platform (Yiddish-sounding, warm, guttural pride)
- **Tessera** — a mosaic design tool (rolling syllables, geometric implication)
- **Zephyr** — a lightweight framework (breathy, fast, vanishingly light)
- **Onyx** — a security product (short, dark, hard, impenetrable)

## 2. Anti-Naming Patterns

| 🚫 Pattern | Why It's Dead |
|---|---|
| Removing vowels (Flickr, Tumblr, Grindr) | A 2005-2010 pattern. Now it signals "we couldn't get the full domain." |
| Adding -ly (Grammarly, Bitly, Assembly) | Pattern exhausted. Hundreds of -ly names exist. You're blending in. |
| Adding -ify (Spotify, Shopify, Testify) | Same as -ly. The suffix adds nothing anymore. |
| Adding -io (Notion.io, Figma.io) | The .io TLD was fresh in 2015. Now it's the default "I'm a startup" flag. |
| Generic Latin/Greek roots (Veridian, Nextera, Omnivex) | Sounds corporate and means nothing. Nobody remembers fake Latin. |
| Forced portmanteaus (Branducation, Fitspiration) | If the combination makes people cringe, it's not clever — it's desperate. |
| Names that require explanation ("It's a play on...") | If you have to explain the name, the name doesn't work. |
| Adding "AI" or "GPT" to the name | Technology changes. Your name shouldn't become an artifact of this specific era. |

---

## 3. Name Generation Checklist

Before finalizing a name, run it through these checks:

- [ ] **Speakability** — Does it sound good when spoken aloud? Say it 5 times fast.
- [ ] **Mental Image** — Does it create a visual in the mind? Can you "see" the name?
- [ ] **Spell Test** — Can someone spell it after hearing it once? No? Change it.
- [ ] **Verb Test** — Can it become a verb? ("Just Google it", "Slack me", "Zoom in") This is the holy grail.
- [ ] **Curiosity Test** — Does the name alone make someone ask "What's that?" Curiosity is the best marketing.
- [ ] **Memory Test** — Would you remember it tomorrow without writing it down?
- [ ] **Domain Check** — Is the .com available? If not, is there a creative TLD that adds meaning?
- [ ] **Emotional Weight** — Does the name carry emotion, or is it neutral? Neutral names are forgotten.
- [ ] **Cultural Check** — Does it mean something unfortunate in another language? Check the major ones.
- [ ] **Uniqueness** — Google the name. If 10 companies already use it, keep going.
